Believe it or not, printing dates back to 3500 BC, with Persian and Mesopotamian civilizations using cylinder seals on clay documents. Woodblock printing and movable type paved the way until the invention of the printing press in 1440.

Charles Babbage designed the first computer printer in the 19th century. The printer was created for his automatic mechanical calculator, The Difference Engine, which was invented to produce error-free tables. However, the printer designed for this machine was not built until the 2000s. The printer was completed by the Science Museum in London in 2000.

The first electronic printer was EP-101, created by the Japanese company Epson in 1968. The printer was only 164 millimeters in width, 102 millimeters in height, and 135 millimeters in depth. It weighed just over 2.5 kilograms.

Email soon came along in the 1990s and 2000s, replacing the need for printing, and the advent of online storage systems meant that physical backups had little benefit.In today's world, even physical printers for offline reading have been replaced by e-books and tablets. While printers are not widely used for documents nowadays, they are considered crucial for more specific specialist purposes like printing photography and artwork. 3D printing is the next stage in printer development, allowing people to create physical objects.
